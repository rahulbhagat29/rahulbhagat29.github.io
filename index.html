Retail Executive Revenue & Margin Performance Dashboard

Retail Domain  |  2026

A multi-category retail business had steady revenue growth from 2022–2024 but lacked visibility into whether growth was sustainable, margin-efficient, and evenly distributed across products, regions, and customer segments. Leadership could not answer critical financial stability questions from raw data alone.

Business Decision Informed: Confirmed $6.55M revenue maintained a stable ~27.7% profit margin throughout growth — proving growth is margin-efficient, not volume-inflated. Discount clustering showed no severe sub-category erosion but flagged as a recurring KPI for the finance function to monitor quarterly.

Built a 3-page executive dashboard with a star schema data model. DAX measures covered Total Revenue, Profit Margin %, YoY Growth, MoM Growth, and Top/Bottom N product rankings using safe DIVIDE logic. Evaluated customer revenue concentration and regional performance distribution.

Customer Retention & Revenue Concentration Analysis

E-Commerce Domain  |  2026

An e-commerce business faced unsustainable customer acquisition costs. Leadership needed to determine whether revenue growth relied on repeat customers or constant new acquisition — a critical input for allocating marketing budget and reducing revenue volatility.

Business Decision Informed: Found the business was acquisition-dependent with high one-time buyer concentration — a direct risk to revenue stability. Pareto analysis confirmed extreme revenue concentration in a small customer subset. Recommended shifting budget from acquisition to a VIP retention programme as the highest-ROI growth lever.

Used multi-table SQL joins across Customers, Orders, and Order Items tables with business-safe filters to exclude cancelled and test transactions. Classified customers in Python into One-Time Buyers vs Repeat Customers. Conducted Pareto revenue concentration analysis and built executive-ready visualisations.

Retail Revenue Analysis & Finance-Safe KPI Dashboard

Retail Domain  |  2025

Raw retail transaction data contained refunds, zero-quantity rows, and pricing mismatches — making revenue numbers unreliable for business decision-making. An executive-ready dashboard required finance-safe KPI definitions before any visualisation could be trusted.

Business Decision Informed: Strong net-to-gross revenue alignment confirmed pricing discipline and minimal leakage. Significant regional performance gaps and high sales volatility flagged as two priority areas for operational investigation. Product category concentration identified as a structural dependency risk requiring diversification strategy.

Defined and calculated three finance-safe KPIs — Gross Sales, Net Revenue, and Refund Impact — treating each data anomaly as a business logic decision rather than a removal problem. Built an executive dashboard with trend, risk, and performance views structured for a finance audience.
